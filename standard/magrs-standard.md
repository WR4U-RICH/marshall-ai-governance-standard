# MAGRS Standard (v0.2)

## Marshall AI Governance Readiness Standard

The Marshall AI Governance Readiness Standard (MAGRS) is a lightweight operational framework designed to help organizations maintain human accountability as artificial intelligence becomes integrated into everyday work.

---

## Foundation

MAGRS is built on the Marshall Principle:

Artificial intelligence may assist human decision-making, but responsibility always remains with humans.

---

## Core Components

MAGRS is structured around three operational pillars:

### 1. Visibility

Organizations maintain awareness of where artificial intelligence is being used.

This includes:

- identifying AI-assisted tools in workflows  
- understanding how outputs are used  
- recognizing where AI influences decisions  

---

### 2. Boundaries

Organizations define appropriate limits for AI-assisted work.

This includes:

- determining acceptable use cases  
- restricting sensitive data exposure  
- clarifying where AI should not be relied upon  

---

### 3. Accountability

Human responsibility for decisions is never delegated to the machine.

This includes:

- assigning responsibility for AI-assisted outputs  
- ensuring human review of critical decisions  
- maintaining clear ownership of outcomes  

---

## Participation Model

Organizations participate in MAGRS through a structured lifecycle:

### Step 1 — Readiness Assessment

Organizations complete an initial assessment to evaluate awareness and current practices.

### Step 2 — Participation

Organizations formally adopt the framework and commit to maintaining governance awareness.

### Step 3 — Quarterly Reporting

Organizations submit periodic reports reflecting on:

- AI usage  
- governance practices  
- responsibility boundaries  

---

## Levels

MAGRS supports progressive levels of participation:

- Level 1 — Initial Participation  
- Level 2 — Sustained Participation  

---

## Intent of the Standard

MAGRS is designed to be:

- practical  
- lightweight  
- accessible to small and mid-sized organizations  

It is not intended to replace regulatory frameworks or technical AI governance systems.

Instead, it provides an operational layer of awareness and responsibility.

---

## Status

MAGRS is currently in **Founder Phase (v0.2)** and evolving through real-world application and feedback.
