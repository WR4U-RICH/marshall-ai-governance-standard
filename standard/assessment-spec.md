# MAGRS Assessment Specification

## Purpose

The MAGRS Readiness Assessment is designed to help organizations reflect on how artificial intelligence is currently being used within their operations and to evaluate their level of governance awareness.

The assessment serves as the entry point into the Marshall AI Governance Readiness Standard (MAGRS).

---

## Assessment Objectives

The assessment evaluates three core areas:

- Visibility
- Boundaries
- Accountability

---

## Core Questions

### Visibility

- Are AI tools currently being used within the organization?
- Does leadership have awareness of where AI is being used?
- Are AI-assisted workflows identified?

---

### Boundaries

- Are there defined limits on how AI tools may be used?
- Are there restrictions on sensitive data exposure?
- Are there areas where AI use is prohibited?

---

### Accountability

- Are individuals responsible for AI-assisted outputs clearly identified?
- Are important decisions reviewed by humans?
- Is responsibility retained by human actors?

---

## Scoring Model (Simple)

The assessment is not intended to produce a technical score.

Instead, it provides a qualitative reflection:

- Low Awareness
- Developing Awareness
- Established Awareness

---

## Outcome

Upon completion of the assessment:

- The organization receives a readiness summary
- The organization is presented with the opportunity to participate in MAGRS
- A unique identifier may be assigned upon participation

---

## Role in the Framework

The assessment serves as:

- the entry point into MAGRS  
- a baseline for governance awareness  
- a reference point for future quarterly reporting  

---

## Alignment

The assessment aligns directly with:

- the MAGRS Standard  
- the Quarterly Readiness Report  
- the Participation Model  

---
